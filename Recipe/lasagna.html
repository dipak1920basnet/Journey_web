Momo

Desciption:

In Tibetan and Nepali cuisine, momos are a sort of steamed filled dumpling that are also well-liked in neighboring Bhutan and India. While most momos from Nepal are spherical, like baozi, most momos from Tibet are half-moon shaped, like jiaozi.
The sauce known as achar, which is typically eaten with momos, is inspired by the spices and herbs found in many South Asian cuisines.
It can also be prepared as soups, called jhol momo or mokthuk, from boiling pork or buffalo bones combined with a variety of herbs and vegetables, or as achar, a broth created from tomatoes, sesame seeds, chillies, cumin, and coriander.
